PTE Academic

Pearson Test of English (PTE) Academic is a computer-based academic English language test aimed at non-native English speakers wanting to study abroad. Recognized by universities, colleges and governments around the world, PTE Academic delivers fast results (typically within 2 business days).

Why Choose PTE Academic?

With completely computer-based testing and scoring, PTE Academic offers unbiased results and fast turnaround. Our course helps you master the test format and question types to maximize your score potential.

Course Structure

  • Speaking & Writing (77-93 minutes): Personal introduction, read aloud, repeat sentence, describe image, re-tell lecture, answer short question, summarize written text, and essay writing.
  • Reading (32-41 minutes): Multiple choice questions, re-order paragraphs, and fill in the blanks (reading and writing).
  • Listening (45-57 minutes): Summarize spoken text, multiple choice questions, fill the blanks, highlight correct summary, select missing word, highlight incorrect words, and write from dictation.

Course Features

  • 50+ hours of comprehensive training (3 weeks full-time or 6 weeks part-time)
  • 8 full-length computer-based mock tests
  • Focus on all 20 different question types
  • Score-boosting strategies for each section
  • Small batch sizes (maximum 6 students per batch)
  • Flexible scheduling options
  • Official PTE practice materials and resources
  • Detailed performance analytics
  • Access to AI-powered scoring tools
  • Test-taking strategies for computer-based format

Key Advantages of PTE

  • Fast results - typically within 2 business days
  • Unlimited score reports to institutions
  • Frequent test dates available
  • Fair and unbiased computer scoring
  • Accepted by thousands of academic programs worldwide
  • Approved for Australian and New Zealand visa applications

Who Should Attend

  • Students applying to universities that accept PTE scores
  • Professionals needing English certification for work visas
  • Applicants for Australian and New Zealand immigration
  • Those who prefer computer-based testing over face-to-face speaking exams
